What is the Bitcoin halving?

Every 210,000 blocks, Bitcoin's code automatically cuts the block reward in half. This event is called the halving (sometimes called the halvening).

The block reward is how new bitcoins enter circulation. Miners compete to add new blocks to the blockchain, and the winner receives a reward in bitcoin. When the halving happens, that reward drops by 50%.

This is not a decision made by any company, government, or person. It is written into Bitcoin's code and has been executing automatically since block 210,000 in 2012. No one can stop it, change it, or override it.

Why does the halving exist?

Bitcoin was designed with a hard cap of 21 million coins. The halving is the mechanism that enforces a predictable, diminishing issuance schedule. By cutting new supply in half every four years, Bitcoin becomes increasingly scarce over time.

Compare this to gold mining: as more gold is found and mined, the supply increases. Gold's scarcity is uncertain. Bitcoin's scarcity is mathematically guaranteed - it is in the code.

How does the timing work?

Bitcoin targets one block every 10 minutes. At that rate, 210,000 blocks takes approximately 4 years. The exact date shifts slightly because miners adjust the difficulty level, which affects how fast blocks actually come in.

The countdown on this page uses live block height data from mempool.space. We calculate the estimated time remaining by multiplying the blocks remaining by 10 minutes per block.

Why do people pay attention to the halving?

Each halving cuts the rate at which new bitcoin enters circulation. Historically, reduced supply issuance combined with steady or growing demand has preceded significant price appreciation in the 12-18 months following each halving.

This is not a guarantee. Markets are complex. But the halving is one of the few economic events in any asset class that is fully predictable and scheduled years in advance. Every market participant can see it coming.

After the 5th halving in 2028, the block reward will drop to 1.5625 BTC. By the year 2140, all 21 million bitcoins will have been mined and the reward will reach zero. At that point, miners will only earn transaction fees.
